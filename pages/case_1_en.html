Building value with Design System:
Scaling and accelerating deliveries in a modular system.

Context and challenge

Although Sebrae already had a Design System in Figma and Storybook, the Empretec product required the creation of a new system, due to specific needs not covered by the existing DS.

With the product underway and an intense routine of meetings, tests, and deliveries, the main challenge was to develop new components without compromising the workflow.

I identified inconsistencies and recurrent rework, which reinforced the need to structure a more appropriate Design System for the project's reality.

Objectives

In addition to concerns about aesthetics/layout, there is also concern about the efficiency of new components and communication with the entire team.
Therefore, the objectives are:

Reduce rework and corrections
↳ Identify and create clear and reusable patterns to avoid rework, misalignment, and inconsistencies.

Ensure visual and functional consistency
↳ Standardize components so that screens follow a unified language, even with different deliveries.

Optimize production time
↳ Reduce repetitive decisions by creating a base of ready-made components, accelerating the design flow.

Improve communication with the development team
↳ Document components clearly to facilitate handoff and avoid misinterpretations.

Increase project scalability
↳ Create a solid foundation that allows for future evolutions without the need to redo components from scratch.

Adapt the Design System to the product's specific needs
↳ Ensure that the system reflects Empretec's particularities, without exclusively depending on the generic DS of the organization.

Problems and solutions

Typeface family

The existing Design System included five different fonts, which were not aligned with the Empretec Brandbook.

As the official font for the project is the Inter (not yet provided in the DS), I started creating a new Design System based on typographic standardization.
Previous fonts were removed, and Inter was incorporated as the sole official font.

To facilitate future adjustments, I created the typography variables.

Components - Typography

Since the previous typeface family did not include the Inter font, the existing components did not follow the Brandbook guidelines.

After including Inter as the main font, and thanks to the structured componentization, it was possible to automatically update all components via the master component.

Components - New

In addition to typographic adjustments, it was necessary to create several exclusive components for Empretec, considering the specificities of its four modules (Urban, Rural, Arena, and Startup).

Each module demanded specific color definitions and some elements, however, headers, footers, inputs, buttons, among others, followed the same patterns, ensuring consistent visual identity and functionality in each context.

Color palette

The absence of Empretec's color palette in the DS required manual application of colors, copied in isolated areas in the file, which compromised the agility of the process and generated visual inconsistencies.

These inconsistencies, which occurred at times, were identified during tests or development, causing rework and affecting team productivity.

To solve the problem, the new palette was structured as variables in Figma and documented in a dedicated tab, promoting standardization, quick access, and alignment between design and development.

Results obtained

Standardized Typography

After standardizing the master components, typographic inconsistencies no longer occurred during testing and development.
As a result, no further adjustments were necessary, ensuring 100% utilization of the time invested in this stage.

Optimized Development: Zero Rework

Componentizing on-screen elements significantly reduced errors, making them virtually nonexistent.
In specific cases (due to the product’s continuous evolution), it’s necessary to temporarily decomponentize for particular adjustments.
When these needs become recurring, a new component is created and integrated into the Design System, ensuring scalability and maintaining consistency.

Accessible and Scalable Color Palette

With the inclusion of the new color palette in variables and styles in Figma, manual application and loose color references in the file were eliminated.
The creation of centralized styles resulted in significant productivity gains, faster access to colors, and an almost complete reduction in visual inconsistencies.

Structured Figma, Aligned Communication!

With the Design System defined and Figma pages organized by theme (such as colors, typography, components, among others), communication became more efficient.
The clear and segmented structure made information more accessible, reducing search time and improving alignment among all stakeholders.

Design System as a foundation for scale and alignment

The Design System was created in Figma because it is a collaborative, flexible tool widely adopted by the team.

Clear and centralized documentation in Figma reduced recurring doubts, accelerated handoff, and improved communication between design, product, and tech teams. This ensured greater visual consistency, time savings on deliveries, and better alignment in interface decisions. Although the Design System solved the main problems, it continues to evolve and adapt to new product demands.

As the product grows and new features are developed, specific needs arise that require the creation or adaptation of components, reinforcing the importance of keeping the DS as a living and iterative system.

Conclusion

This project is a clear example of my commitment to creating design solutions that not only visually delight, but also deliver strategic value and tangible results. By leading the restructuring and improvement of the Empretec Design System, I demonstrated how standardization and consistency can optimize processes, reducing rework and inconsistencies, and improving communication between teams.

My approach focused on scalability and adaptability ensured that the system became a living asset, capable of boosting productivity and agility in the development of new functionalities, resulting in a more robust and effective final product.
